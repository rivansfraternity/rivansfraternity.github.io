Rivans Fraternity Recurring Events

The Rivans Fraternity, officially known as the Rivulet Sigma Omega Beta Society, Inc., observes several meaningful traditions and recurring events throughout the year. These events serve to strengthen the bond of brotherhood, promote community service, and uphold the organization's core values of Leadership and Fellowship.

Signature Annual Event

Founding Anniversary Celebration

Date: Every November 18

Significance: This event commemorates the founding of the Rivans Fraternity on November 18, 1978 in Pagadian City by our respected Founder, Kap Ronald "Roy" L. Gillamac.

As the largest and most important gathering of the year, the Founding Anniversary typically includes a motorcade, alumni homecoming, fellowship activities, and a rededication to the fraternity’s guiding motto: “Huwag mang-api at huwag magpa-api.”

Other Recurring Events

Aside from the Founding Anniversary, the fraternity also regularly holds other annual activities such as:

  • Anniversary Motorcade – A symbolic procession usually held in November.
  • Alumni Homecoming – A reunion that brings together past and present members.
  • Community Service Projects – Regular outreach and service activities conducted throughout the year.

These recurring events are vital in keeping the spirit of the Rivans Fraternity alive across all chapters.
